The Bait: Promises of Crypto Payouts & VIPs

I joined Metawin’s affiliate program late last year, negotiating directly with their representative, "Phil." The appeal was clear: crypto-based payouts for affiliates, a decent rev-share, and the ability to bring high-value VIPs from other major sites. My very first referral was a real-life friend (my brother-in-law) who signed up from my own house, played regularly, and deposited as agreed.

At the outset, there was a mandatory two-month waiting period before they paid out the $100 referral bonus. I waited. But after that, I checked my affiliate panel—and my referred player had disappeared without so much as an email or notification. With no onsite support, I spent days chasing Discord reps until I finally got Phil’s email.

He claimed there was a "shared IP" concern (despite full explanations and proof of the player's IRL relationship and his home address). After weeks of going in circles, they eventually reinstated my player and paid me the bonus, plus a single month of rev-share (in crypto, as promised). For a moment, I thought it was sorted.

The Switch: When the Money Got Real

Once my rev-share balance began to grow over the following two months, things suddenly changed:

  • Metawin stopped all further crypto payments, demanding fiat banking info and KYC (nowhere in the original agreement).
  • When I asked for the crypto option, I got silence or deflection.
  • They suddenly revived the “self-referral” excuse, despite already investigating and clearing it before paying me.
  • Support became virtually unreachable—Telegram admins and Discord reps just passed the buck.
  • My affiliate dashboard broke, making it impossible to track earnings or referrals.

The VIP Player Betrayal

At the same time, I’d brought in two high-roller players from Stake/Shuffle, under explicit agreements for their Salon Rouge VIP status:
